# Suspected fall

Falls are common and can be serious for older adults with dementia. If a fall is suspected, the
priority is to avoid causing further injury and to get help.

## Immediate steps
- Do not move the person if any injury is suspected, especially to the head, neck, back, or hip.
- Assess responsiveness: are they awake, able to answer, and breathing normally?
- Ask about and look for pain, bleeding, or an obviously injured or misshapen limb.
- Keep the person warm, calm, and still while you summon help.

## When it is an emergency
Call emergency services if the person:
- Is unconscious, drowsy, or cannot be woken.
- Hit their head, or is on blood-thinning medication and may have hit their head.
- Has severe pain, cannot move or bear weight, or has a possible broken bone.
- Is confused in a new or worsening way after the fall.

## After a fall
- Even a minor fall with new confusion warrants urgent review, because it can signal infection,
  dehydration, medication effects, or injury.
- Record the fall — time, place, and possible cause (rug, poor lighting, footwear, dizziness) —
  so repeat hazards can be removed.
