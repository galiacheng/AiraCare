# Home safety and wandering prevention

A safe home reduces both accidents and the anxiety that drives many incidents. Prevention is far
better than reacting to a fall or an exit at night.

## Reduce fall hazards
- Remove loose rugs, trailing cords, and clutter from walking paths.
- Improve lighting, especially on stairs and on the route to the bathroom; use night lights.
- Ensure sturdy footwear and, where advised, grab rails in the bathroom.
- Keep frequently used items within easy reach to avoid climbing or overreaching.

## Reduce wandering and exit risk
- Fit door chimes or sensors so a caregiver is alerted when a door opens.
- Reduce visual cues that prompt leaving — keep coats, keys, and shoes out of direct sight near
  exits.
- Provide identification the person wears or carries, and consider a location-aware device if
  wandering is a known risk.

## Support orientation and calm
- Keep a predictable routine and familiar objects in view.
- Use clear signage or labels (for example on the bathroom door) if it helps.
- Meet daytime needs for activity, daylight, and social contact, which lowers evening
  restlessness and exit-seeking.
