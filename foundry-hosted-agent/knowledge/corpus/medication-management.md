# Medication management and missed doses

People with dementia may forget doses, take them twice, or resist medication. Safe handling
protects against both under- and over-dosing.

## A missed dose
- Follow the prescriber's or pharmacist's guidance for that specific medicine. Never double up to
  "catch up" unless explicitly told to.
- If unsure whether a dose was taken, do not give another dose on a guess; check the pill
  organiser or log first.
- Record the miss and watch for symptom changes, especially for medicines that control mood,
  seizures, heart, or diabetes.

## Reducing errors
- Use a dated pill organiser or blister pack so "taken or not" is visible at a glance.
- Keep a simple medication log with time given and who gave it.
- Give medicines at consistent times tied to daily routine (meals, bedtime).

## If the person refuses
- Do not force medication. Try again a short while later with a calm approach.
- Check for a reason: difficulty swallowing, a bad taste, suspicion, or feeling unwell.
- Persistent refusal of important medication is worth a caregiver or clinician review rather than
  a daily struggle.
